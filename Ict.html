KAKUMA REFUGEE CAMP

Kakuma Refugee camp was formed in the year 1992 with the displaced community from south sudan due to heavy in war in Sudan because of the khartoum government who led the troop power in marginalizing other ethnic groups in Sudan. Today Kakuma is not only comprised of south Sudanese people but with multiple groups from the neighboring countries. Click here to learn more about kakuma.

KAKUMA REFUGEE CAMP

Kakuma is founded by UNHCR to support the refugee programs in the camp since its establishment, some of the programs include Education, Resettlement, provision of basic needs which includes food, water, and shelter and Scholarship. Visit our youtube channel.

INTER-CULTURAL ACTIVIITES PRACTICED IN KAKUMA REFUGEE CAMP.

Today Kakuma is famous with inter-cultural activities such as sport, transport, agriculture, business activities and vocational programs. Since kakuma has become a home to thousands of many refugees who felt out of returning to their home town due to many of the circumstance. The number of refugees has rised to 105 = 100000, due to this number, refugees have experience some of the challenges such as lack of H20 and food.
